About me

Hiiii!!! I teach cameras and algorithms to understand farms… on good days, they even listen. My work emphasizes agricultural robotics, computer vision, and deep learning to address real-world challenges in agricultural automation and sensing.

I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Scholar at Michigan State University. My work focuses on developing AI- and vision-enabled systems for smart agriculture.

Before joining MSU, I have been serving as an Agricultural Research Service (ARS) Scientist at the ICAR–Research Complex for North Eastern Hill (NEH) Region, Umiam, Meghalaya, India (July 2018–present; currently on leave). Here, I have contributed to mechanization solutions for hill agriculture.

I completed my Ph.D. (2023) in Agricultural Robotics from Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur, West Bengal, India. Over the years, my work has spanned robotic harvesting systems, vision-based crop perception, and AI-driven sensing, aiming to translate research into deployable solutions.

Outside research, I enjoy playing lawn tennis and recharge with music and movies—especially anything with Irrfan Khan (my all-time favorite).

Research

My research sits at the intersection of agricultural robotics, computer vision, and AI-driven sensing, with a strong focus on field-deployable systems that reduce labor requirements and improve automation in farms.

Agricultural Robotics & Field Automation

  • Robotic harvesting systems (Ph.D. work on cotton boll harvesting robotics).
  • End-to-end outdoor pipeline mindset: perception → decision → actuation for unstructured fields.
  • Smart Mechanization solutions specialized for hill agriculture (design + practical deployment focus).

Computer Vision for Crops

  • Deep learning for crop/plant part detection and segmentation in real field conditions.
  • Vision transformer and CNN-based perception methods for crop objects (e.g., cotton bolls).

AI-Enabled Optical Sensing

  • NIR spectroscopy + AI for rapid, non-destructive trait prediction and germplasm screening.
  • Transformer and deep learning models for protein/quality prediction from spectra.

Mobile & Digital Tools for Farmers

  • Android apps translating models into usable, on-field decision support (deployment-oriented).
  • Real-time, user-friendly interfaces that reduce the gap between research and adoption.

Current Directions

  • Developing vision-integrated robotic harvesting systems for crops and vegetables.
  • Integrating AI in agriculture and allied sciences to automate precision farming.
  • Reducing post-harvest losses and drudgery in hill agriculture via smart mechanization.
  • Scalable spectral/vision screening tools for rapid phenotyping and quality assessment.
